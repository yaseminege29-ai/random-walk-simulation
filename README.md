# 2D Random Walk Simulation

This project explores the behavior of a particle undergoing a two-dimensional random walk and analyzes its motion using Mean Squared Displacement (MSD).

## Overview

A random walk is a fundamental model in statistical physics used to describe stochastic processes such as Brownian motion and diffusion. In this project, I simulated the motion of a particle taking random steps in two dimensions and studied how its displacement evolves over time.

## What This Project Includes

- Simulation of 2D random motion using Gaussian-distributed steps  
- Calculation of Mean Squared Displacement (MSD)  
- Averaging over multiple runs to reduce noise  
- Analysis of how MSD evolves with time (number of steps)  
- Comparison of MSD for different step counts  

## Key Observation

The results show that the Mean Squared Displacement increases approximately linearly with time, which is a characteristic signature of diffusive processes.

## Physics Insight

This linear relationship between MSD and time reflects the nature of diffusion, where particle motion is governed by random fluctuations rather than deterministic forces.
